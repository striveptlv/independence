Clinician Teaching Guide

Using This With A Patient

Grocery Shopping Task gives patients a structured online shopping space to practice technology use, organization, attention, budgeting, and safety judgment before returning to real-world grocery routines.

1. Choose The Goal

Select the shopping list that matches the patient’s current attention, language, visual scanning, memory, and executive function needs.

2. Set Support Level

Use Therapist Controls to simplify store choice, turn budgets or fees on/off, and adjust substitutions, coupons, hints, aphasia mode, or low-vision mode.

3. Preview The Task

Review the task card together so the patient knows the required items, budget, checkout method, and special rules before shopping.

4. Shop The List

Have the patient search, browse aisles, compare options, add items, monitor the cart total, and check whether the cart matches the goal.

5. Review Checkout

Coach the patient to verify pickup or delivery, fees, tip, discounts, substitutions, extra items, and final total before confirming the order.

6. Debrief And Document

Review missed items, extra items, cueing, corrections, budget accuracy, and safety decisions, then generate a therapist summary and SOAP note.
